The real problem

Your church doesn't lack activity. It lacks clarity for disciple-making.

The church rarely lacks events, programs, or content. Somewhere along the way, the Great Commission quietly got rewritten in practice into something much smaller:

"Go into all the world and make more worship attenders, baptizing them into the name of small groups, and teaching them to volunteer a few times a month." — the Functional Great Commission, from Will Mancini's Future Church

That's the problem underneath the problems: over-programmed and under-discipled. People connect to your building, your programs, and your personalities — the lower room — and every one of those can change overnight. The upper room is your church's purpose: a unique, disciple-making mission and vision. But in most churches, nobody has ever turned the lights on up there — not because leaders don't care, but because nobody ever gave them the tools or the guided time with their team to do the deep work.

That deep work is exactly what the co::Lab is.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

Who from our church should participate?
Your lead minister plus your key leaders — elders and staff who carry the weight of direction. The process is designed for a "Future Team," not a solo pastor, because alignment is built during the journey, not after it.
What's the actual time commitment?
One 4-hour virtual intensive per month for 10 months, plus team conversations between sessions using the provided exercises. It's designed for real teams with real schedules.
We're bigger than 200 — can we still join?
Yes. The cohort is tuned for churches around 200 or less, but larger churches have walked this process with great fruit. Talk with Andrew about fit for your context.
How does the grant pricing work?
The co::Lab is normally $5,000 per church. Through RunFree's partnership with CCL Network and their Lilly Endowment grant, every church in this cohort participates for $2,500 — billed as $250/month across the 10 months.
What do we walk away with?
Two celebrated deliverables: your Vision Frame (mission, values, strategy, measures, and vision — your church's unique DNA, codified) and your Horizon Storyline (a long-range, God-sized dream with a short-term executable plan). Plus a leadership team that's actually aligned around both.
Is this just another vision statement exercise?
No. Wordsmithing a statement is the smallest part. The Pivvot process addresses the paradigm underneath — moving the finish line from attendance to disciple-making — and then codifies vision that your team owns because they built it.
What if we miss a session or our plans change?
Life happens in ministry — reach out to Andrew and the team will work with you. The online community and digital resources keep your team moving between and around sessions.
